Ontology of Medical Devices Regulated under the European MDR/IVDR Framework: Description back to ToC

The ontology of medical devices regulated under the European MDR/IVDR framework is designed to structure and represent information about medical devices, especially high-risk devices and in vitro diagnostic devices. This ontology models the relationship between the main elements of the domain: the devices, the manufacturers, the identified risks, the safety corrective actions, and the reported incidents. This makes it possible to keep all the information organized in one place, making it more accessible and useful for everyone.

Rule1back to ToC or SWRL ToC

Infers that a device is an in vitro diagnostic device when it is regulated under the IVDR framework

Device c(?d) , hasRegulatoryFramework op(?d,?rf) , IVDR c(?rf) -> InVitroDiagnosticDevice c(?d)

Rule2back to ToC or SWRL ToC

Infers that corrective action has been taken for an incident when the linked corrective action has closed status

Incident c(?i) , hasCorrectiveAction op(?i,?a) , hasStatus op(?a,?s) , Closed c(?s) -> corrective_action_taken dp(?i,"true"^^boolean)

Rule3back to ToC or SWRL ToC

Infers that a risk belongs to the device failure category when its risk type is malfunction risk

Risk c(?r) , hasRiskType op(?r,?t) , MalfunctionRisk c(?t) -> DeviceFailure c(?r)
